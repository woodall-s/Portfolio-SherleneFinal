Website Redesign

A redesign of a nonprofit organization website that needed more visible donation information.

Project Overview

Duration

2 Weeks

Format

Website and Mobile

Team

Ashley, Samuel, Sherlene

Tools

InVision, FigJam, Figma

Project Overview

Visitors to nonprofit websites want donation transparency. Knowing exactly how their donation is used increases their trust in the organization and the likelihood that they will donate. So, the question for our team was: how might we make finding detailed donation information on our site easy for visitors?

User Research

Our team began our user research by creating a proto-persona to discover the type of users who would most likley be visiting our site. From there, we developed our User Research plan included questions based on the cause of organization and our problem statement. We interviewed several people that that fall between the ages of 25-50, from different racial backgrounds. During our interviews, we realized that people had no issues with donating to smaller organizations. Interviewees mentioned they wanted more transparency as to where the funds were being allocated.

Definition & Synthesis

Our Affinity Diagram revealed that our users are experienced donators who prefer supporting small non-profits. Transparency is a major concern for them, especially knowing the details of how their donation is spent. We then used the data gathered from the Affinity Diagram to create an Empathy Map and clearly define our User-Persona. We then developed our Value Proposition and mapped out our User's Journey.

Ideation

To get a better understanding of where users feel they should look for certain information, we had a classmate sort cards under the category they saw best fit. We made the decision of where to place our site information and pages after analyzing how our classmate categorized the cards. This lead to the site map we created. When considering the style of the website, we wanted to keep the same feel the organization already had. The whole layout provided a party-like vibe which is reflected in the bright, neon colors, as well as the fun, yet sophisticated fonts displayed.

Prototyping

Our biggest hurdle in redesigning this site was finding a way to create content that more efficiently communicated For the Gworls' cause and donation information while staying true to the sites look and feel. Through our iterations, we continued to try to find a balance between the images important to the organization and the content needed. We used photo collages and cards containing images and text to find that balance. Maintaining the party vibe of the original site was important, but we also made sure that selected pages and layouts were designed to drive users to donate to our organization.

User Testing & Outcomes

Our final usability tests revealed that users felt the site was well spaced out and easy to use. Users felt that information provided was sufficient and easy to understand. Users for the most part did not have a problem navigating the site. Finally, users felt comfortable donating to the organization after navigating the site.

Conclusion & Future Opportunities

In conclusion, we feel the site is more informative and provides a more user friendly experience while staying true to the brand. For our next steps, we would include testimonials of those who have benefited from the support of For the Gworls. Additionally, we will possibly create a page dedicated to testimonials, rewiews, and organization stats. Lastly, we would like to add more animations to the homepage.
